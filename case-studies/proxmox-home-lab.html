Executive Summary

A lab foundation for practical systems administration review.

This case study ties the preserved home lab PDF and topology diagram to infrastructure learning outcomes: virtualization, service placement, network boundaries, documentation quality, and operational troubleshooting context.

Business Objective

Show a realistic practice environment for support and infrastructure skills.

Give recruiters and technical reviewers a concise route from project summary to preserved lab evidence without overstating production experience.

Skills Demonstrated
  • Proxmox and virtualization concepts
  • Windows and Linux lab workload awareness
  • Network topology documentation
  • Infrastructure boundary mapping
  • Evidence preservation and reviewer handoff

Technical Environment

Preserved home lab infrastructure context.

The evidence references a Dell R710 and lab workloads, Proxmox concepts, network switching, Active Directory-style services, DNS/DHCP context, Microsoft administration concepts, and PowerShell-supported operations.

Architecture Overview

Topology evidence supports project orientation.

The home lab topology diagram provides a quick visual path for reviewers. The preserved PDF remains supporting evidence for the broader lab portfolio and should be treated as historical proof rather than a current production environment claim.

Implementation Approach

Use lab evidence as context for operational workflows.

The lab supports the surrounding case studies by giving a practical environment for identity, networking, DNS/DHCP, automation, and troubleshooting documentation. Claims stay scoped to preserved lab evidence and documented methodology.

Validation Process

Review topology, proof-library index entries, PDF accessibility from public pages, and related evidence links. Avoid implying current uptime, production traffic, or enterprise service ownership.

Lessons Learned

A home lab is most valuable to employers when it is translated into clear diagrams, support workflows, validation notes, and reusable documentation.
